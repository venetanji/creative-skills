# Suno Style Prompt Guide

The `--tags` field is a **full-sentence producer brief** — not a comma-separated keyword list.

| ✅ Do this | ❌ Not this |
|---|---|
| *"trip-hop, 92 BPM, detuned Rhodes chords over a wobbling sub-bass, shuffled rim clicks, breathy lead vocal with tape delay throws. Opens with crumbling drum machine grooves — the chorus lifts into harmonized vocals before dissolving into a half-spoken bridge."* | *"trip-hop, bass, vocals, drums, slow"* |

---

## Structure

Write it like a producer brief in this order:

1. **Genre/sub-genre** — be specific: "folk noir" not "folk", "trip-hop" not "hip hop"
2. **BPM** — e.g. `92 BPM`, `slow 68 BPM`, `uptempo 128 BPM`
3. **Instruments** — the key ones: `fingerpicked acoustic guitar`, `bowed upright bass`, `detuned Rhodes chords`
4. **Production texture** — `vinyl crackle intro`, `tape delay throws`, `sidechain pumping`, `analog tape saturation`
5. **Vocal style** — `breathy lead vocal`, `half-spoken delivery`, `world-weary baritone`, `harmonized chorus`
6. **Atmosphere/mood** — `nocturnal cityscape`, `minor key melancholy`, `rain-soaked streets`
7. **Narrative sentence** — how the track's energy builds and falls

---

## Examples

**Folk noir / coastal Americana:**
> trip-hop, folk noir, coastal Americana, 74 BPM, fingerpicked acoustic guitar, bowed upright bass, sparse brushed snare, foghorn field recording, salt wind ambience, weathered male vocal, world-weary baritone, close-mic room reverb, minor pentatonic melody, mournful tone. A sparse track built around fingerpicked guitar and bowed bass. The vocal sits close and unadorned over brushed percussion and field recordings of harbour wind. A restrained string swell lifts the chorus before the arrangement dissolves back into silence and tape hiss.

**Cinematic synthwave:**
> cinematic synthwave, 80s retrowave, 110 BPM, analog polysynth arpeggios, driving gated snare, pulsing Moog bass, detuned pad layer, gated reverb, vocoder harmonies, neon-lit melancholy, nocturnal highway atmosphere. Opens on a slow synth pad swell before the gated snare kicks in and the arpeggiator climbs. Vocoder harmonies glide over the chorus as the bass locks into a four-on-the-floor pulse.

**Trip-hop / downtempo:**
> trip-hop, deep house, 92 BPM, sampled ferris wheel ambience, vinyl crackle intro, detuned Rhodes chords, wobbling sub-bass lead, broken kick pattern, shuffled rim clicks, breathy lead vocal, half-spoken delivery, harmonized chorus, tape delay throws, stereo spring reverb, sidechain pumping, analog tape saturation, minor key melancholy. Opens with crumbling drum machine grooves and detuned Rhodes over a pulsing sub-bass. The chorus lifts into harmonized vocals and tape delay before dissolving into a half-spoken bridge.

**Epic dark fantasy / orchestral:**
> dark fantasy orchestral, 78 BPM, sweeping string sections, low brass stabs, war drums, haunting female choir, solo cello melody, deep sub-bass rumble, dramatic dynamic swells, battle tension, ancient mythology atmosphere. Begins with a solitary cello line over silence before the full orchestra erupts. Choir enters on the chorus, building to a crushing brass-and-percussion climax before receding into an eerie string fade.

## Tips

- The narrative sentence at the end tells Suno the **shape** of the track (how energy moves), not just the sound palette — always include it
- Avoid genre words alone ("rock", "pop") — always pair with sub-genre and descriptive details
- Specific instrument names beat generic ones: "detuned Rhodes" beats "piano"
- Production texture words (`vinyl crackle`, `tape delay`, `sidechain`) have strong effect on the output vibe
